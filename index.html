Rice feeds billions of people, weaving itself into the daily lives and cultures of communities around the world. Yet, access to this essential grain is far from equal. Some countries produce more than enough, while others struggle to keep pace with the needs of their growing populations.

To help make sense of this imbalance, this visualization presents the Rice Accessibility Index (RAI), a measure that indicates whether enough rice is produced near where people live to satisfy their consumption needs.

The index is calculated using:
RAI = (Rice Supply ÷ Rice Consumption) × 100

As you explore, you can follow the story of rice accessibility across three different years and see how it intertwines with other forces—GDP per Capita, National and Mean RAI, Production, Total Population, and Consumption—all of which shape how readily rice reaches the people who depend on it.
